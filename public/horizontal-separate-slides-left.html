Welcome to Filmstrip.js

The library designed to make retro-style slideshows

Concept

First slideshows were invented in early 1600s, when hand-painted pictures on a piece of glass were first projected onto a wall using "magic lantern". Sunlight, candles and oil lamps were the only available light sources. The development of light bulbs opened up a world of practical applications for image projection.

Concept

In the 1800s, a series of hand-painted glass "lantern slides" was sometimes projected to illustrate story-telling or a lecture. By 1900, hand-painted images on glass had been replaced by photographic images, but they still needed to be colorized manually.

Concept

After 35 mm Kodachrome color film was introduced in 1936, a new standard 2×2 inch (5×5 cm) miniature lantern slide format was created to better suit the very small transparencies the film produced. In advertising, the antique "magic lantern" terminology was streamlined, so that the framed pieces of film were simply "slides" and the lantern used to project them was a "slide projector".

Concept

A filmstrip (diafilm) is a spooled roll of 35 mm positive film with approximately thirty to fifty images arranged in sequential order. In USSR, diafilms was the most affordable show. Every soviet family could afford to buy a filmstrip projector. There were a lot of soviet diafilms with fairytales and literary works.

Key features

  • Flexible - it allows you to create a presentation that runs right in your browser, no need to install any extra software.
  • Cross-platform - it allows you to view presentations in any browser on any platform.
  • Adjustable - your presentations can fit any screen resolution.

Types of presentations

There are 2 types of presentations supported by this library:

  1. Filmstrip presentations (default option) - your presentation looks like a diafilm filmstrip.
  2. Separate slides - your presentation looks like a diapositive slideshow with separate slides.

Filmstrip presentations

There are 2 options for filmstrip presentations:

  • Vertical (default option) - your presentation looks like a diafilm filmstrip with vertical scrolling.
  • Horizontal - your presentation looks like a diafilm filmstrip with horizontal scrolling.

Separate slides

There are 4 options for separate slides presentations:

  • Down (default option) - all slides move down offscreen when switching.
  • Up - all slides move up offscreen when switching.
  • Left - all slides move left offscreen when switching.
  • Right - all slides move right offscreen when switching.
